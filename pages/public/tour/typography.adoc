*Typography* is the way text looks on a web page -- the fonts, the sizes,
the spacing, and the line height. The J1 Template treats typography as one
of the most important *branding elements* of your website. Good typography
makes a page easier to read and gives your site its own visual character,
almost like the way a logo or an image does.

== Responsive Text

*Responsive text* is text that changes its size *automatically* to fit
the screen. On a big monitor it looks bigger, on a phone it looks smaller
-- but it always stays easy to read.

Many website templates do not support responsive text. They resize images
and boxes, but the text size stays fixed. The J1 Template fixes this. It
adjusts both the font size *and* the line height so reading stays
comfortable on every device.

Below you can see how text behaves with a *fixed* size -- the way most
templates show it. Click the *Toggle rtext* button to switch between
fixed text and responsive text, then change the size of your browser
window to see the difference.

== Text Scaling

Responsive text comes in *five sizes* (called scale factors), from 100
to 500. With these sizes you can:

* use responsive text everywhere on your pages, and
* offer different text sizes to visitors who need a larger or smaller
  font, without asking them to change their browser settings.

Click the *Scale rtext* button below to switch the text size from level
300 to level 500 and back. Try resizing your browser window at the same
time to see how the text always stays easy to read.

++++

    Scale rtext
  </button>

== Scale factors

Responsive text comes in *five* sizes, named `r-text-100` through
`r-text-500`. The smallest is `r-text-100`, the largest is `r-text-500`.

Behind the scenes, the sizes are based on the *Fibonacci series* -- a
famous sequence of numbers that often appears in nature and in design.
The Fibonacci numbers give pleasing proportions, which is why they are
also popular in typography. The J1 Template translates these numbers
into CSS rules so the text scales smoothly with the browser size.
